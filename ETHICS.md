# Ethics Statement

## Research Scope

This repository contains theoretical threat modeling research on novel AI attack vectors. All research is conducted and published strictly for **defensive and academic purposes**.

## What This Research Is

- Conceptual framework development identifying previously undescribed attack surfaces
- Threat model formalization intended to inform defensive system design
- Proposed technique contributions to the MITRE ATLAS adversarial ML taxonomy
- Educational materials to raise awareness among developers, platform operators, and financial regulators

## What This Research Is Not

- No actual attacks were conducted at any stage of this research
- No proof-of-concept exploit code exists in this repository
- No real financial markets, AI systems, or individuals were targeted
- No empirical validation of attack effectiveness was attempted

All scenarios described are abstract theoretical models. The absence of empirical validation is explicitly acknowledged in the Limitations sections of each paper.

## Why We Publish

The attack surfaces described in this research — supply chain poisoning of AI models, API proxy interception, and cognitive drift via multi-agent interaction — exist independently of this publication. Responsible disclosure of theoretical attack vectors allows:

- Developers to assess their own toolchains for exposure
- Platform operators to design countermeasures before incidents occur
- Financial regulators to update frameworks proactively
- The research community to develop detection and mitigation methods

Withholding this analysis does not eliminate the attack surface. It only delays the defensive response.

## Responsible Use

This research is published under **TLP:WHITE** and **CC BY-NC-ND 4.0**.

Anyone using this research:
- May cite and reference it for academic, educational, and defensive security purposes
- May not use it to design, implement, or facilitate actual attacks
- May not create derivative works for commercial purposes without permission

This research is independent work conducted without institutional affiliation or external funding. No conflicts of interest exist. The author has no financial positions in any securities markets discussed in the theoretical scenarios.
